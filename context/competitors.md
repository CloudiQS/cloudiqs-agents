# Competitor Intelligence

## Primary competitors

### Cloudreach (Sandstone Technology Group)
**Type:** AWS Premier Partner, large SI
**Size:** ~1,000 employees, global
**Strengths:** Brand recognition, large delivery teams, enterprise relationships
**Weaknesses:** Expensive, slow, junior delivery teams on SMB accounts, minimum engagement sizes £100K+
**How to win:** Speed, cost, senior engineer access. We move faster and the customer gets our principals, not graduates.
**ACE field:** `Cloudreach` → use `Other- Cost Optimization` or `On-Prem` as primary competitor where applicable

---

### Rackspace
**Type:** AWS Premier Partner, managed hosting + cloud
**Size:** ~6,000 employees, public company
**Strengths:** Large support organisation, 24/7 operations, brand
**Weaknesses:** Expensive lock-in contracts, poor developer experience, slow to adopt new AWS services, US-centric processes
**How to win:** Flexibility, AWS-native tooling, no proprietary layer. We build on open standards, they sell proprietary monitoring.
**ACE field:** `Rackspace` — not a standard ACE enum, use `Other- Cost Optimization`

---

### 6point6 (part of Accenture)
**Type:** AWS Advanced Partner, digital transformation SI
**Size:** ~300 employees (pre-acquisition), now part of Accenture
**Strengths:** Public sector relationships, government framework contracts, strategy consulting
**Weaknesses:** Now part of Accenture overhead, less agile, expensive, focused on public sector
**How to win:** Private sector agility, no framework delays, outcome-based not time-and-materials
**ACE field:** `Other- Cost Optimization`

---

### Contino (part of Cognizant)
**Type:** DevOps/cloud transformation consultancy
**Size:** ~400 employees, acquired by Cognizant
**Strengths:** Engineering culture, platform engineering expertise, DevOps transformation
**Weaknesses:** Now Cognizant subsidiary, expensive, focused on large enterprises, minimum team sizes
**How to win:** SMB-appropriate sizing, fixed-price projects, we do not require a dedicated platform team
**ACE field:** `Other- Cost Optimization`

---

### Nordcloud (IBM company)
**Type:** AWS Premier Partner, multi-cloud MSP
**Size:** ~700 employees, acquired by IBM
**Strengths:** Strong in Nordics and mainland Europe, large MSP operations, IBM backing
**Weaknesses:** IBM overhead, multi-cloud dilutes AWS focus, not UK-native, minimum contract sizes
**How to win:** AWS-only focus, UK-first, no IBM bureaucracy
**ACE field:** `Other- Cost Optimization`

---

### Claranet
**Type:** UK MSP, multi-cloud
**Size:** ~1,500 employees, private equity backed
**Strengths:** Strong UK sales, existing mid-market relationships, broad service portfolio
**Weaknesses:** Multi-cloud generalist (not AWS-specialist), legacy MSP tooling, not AWS-certified at scale
**How to win:** AWS competencies, MAP funding access (they cannot offer this as non-Advanced), engineering depth
**ACE field:** `Other- Cost Optimization`

## Internal competition: AWS Professional Services

Sometimes AWS PS is involved in a deal. This is not a competitor situation — AWS PS works with partners. If AWS PS is engaged, it is an opportunity to position as the delivery partner for ongoing operations post-project.

---

## Competitor handling rules

- Never bad-mouth a competitor by name in cold outreach
- If a prospect mentions a competitor, acknowledge and pivot: "Good to know — what is working well with them and what would you like to be different?"
- In ACE opportunities: use the `Competitor` field accurately. AWS uses this for market intelligence. Common values:
  - `No Competition` — no other vendors in consideration
  - `On-Prem` — they are considering staying on-prem
  - `Microsoft Azure` — Azure is the alternative
  - `Google Cloud Platform` — GCP is the alternative
  - `Other- Cost Optimization` — another AWS partner or SI
  - `Co-location` — they are considering a colo instead

## Deal intelligence signals

If a prospect mentions one of these competitors, they are likely further along in the buying process:
- Cloudreach → they have budget and are comparing proposals
- Rackspace → they are unhappy with current state and looking
- Contino → they have a platform/DevOps challenge, not just lift-and-shift
- Any of the above → accelerate to meeting, do not nurture
