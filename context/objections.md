# Common Objections and Responses

## Objection 1: "We already have an AWS partner"

**What they mean:** They have an existing relationship (often a reseller or large SI) and are not actively looking to switch.

**Response:**
"That is great to hear — it means you are already on AWS, which is exactly where we work best. Most of our customers came to us while already having a partner on record. The question is not whether to switch, it is whether your current partner is actively saving you money and building towards your roadmap. We offer a free Well-Architected Review — no commitment, no sales pitch, just an honest assessment. If your current partner has done that recently, there is nothing we can add. If not, it is worth 2 hours of your time."

**Key tool:** Free Well-Architected Review (MAP-fundable).

---

## Objection 2: "We do not have budget right now"

**What they mean:** Either (a) genuinely no budget cycle open, (b) they are testing us, or (c) they do not see enough value yet.

**Response:**
"Understood. Two things worth knowing: first, if you qualify for MAP funding, AWS covers a significant portion of migration costs — typically £15K–£80K depending on your workload. That changes the budget conversation entirely. Second, our MSP engagements start at £2K/month, which for most companies is covered by the AWS cost savings we find in the first 60 days. We are not asking for budget — we are asking for 30 minutes to show you what funding is available and what savings are realistic."

**Key tool:** MAP funding eligibility check. Run `ace-funding` agent if opportunity exists.

---

## Objection 3: "We are happy managing AWS ourselves"

**What they mean:** They have internal engineers and do not want to outsource. May also mean they underestimate risk.

**Response:**
"Completely fair — and if your team has AWS expertise, we are not here to replace them. What we typically find is that internal teams are excellent at building but stretched on ongoing cost optimisation, security posture, and keeping up with AWS service releases. We have customers where the internal team does all the development and we handle the operational layer: cost reports, security scanning, monthly WAR reviews. It gives the internal team their time back without losing control."

**Key tool:** Co-managed MSP positioning. Lead with security review offer.

---

## Objection 4: "We are in the middle of a project with another consultancy"

**What they mean:** Active engagement elsewhere, not a good time to switch.

**Response:**
"I would not suggest interrupting a live project — that never goes well. What I would suggest is a conversation now so that when the project completes, you have an alternative on the table. The questions to ask your current partner at project close: What is the ongoing support model? What does cost optimisation look like in year 2? Do they have MAP funding secured for the next migration phase? If those answers are not satisfying, we would like to be the next call."

**Key tool:** Nurture sequence. Tag in HubSpot as `end_of_project` and schedule follow-up in 90 days.

---

## Objection 5: "We are going to do this in-house"

**What they mean:** Leadership has decided to hire cloud engineers rather than engage a partner.

**Response:**
"That is a legitimate path. The risk we see is the hiring timeline — a senior AWS architect takes 3–5 months to hire and 3 months to ramp. That is 6–8 months before you have someone operational. In that window, you are paying for infrastructure that is not optimised and running security risk that is not addressed. We offer a bridge: 3–6 month engagement to get the architecture right while you hire. When your engineer starts, they inherit a clean, well-documented AWS environment instead of inheriting technical debt."

**Key tool:** Fixed-term engagement offer. Position as complement to hiring, not competition.

---

## General handling rules

- Never argue. Acknowledge the objection, then reframe.
- Always offer something concrete: a free review, a funding check, a 30-minute call.
- Do not discount. Lead with funding availability instead.
- If they say no twice, tag for nurture and move on. Do not chase.
- Email tone: no contractions, no em dashes. Sound like a human, not a template.
